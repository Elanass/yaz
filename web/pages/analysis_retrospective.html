About Retrospective Analysis

Retrospective analysis examines historical patient data to identify factors associated with outcomes. This approach is valuable for hypothesis generation and identifying potential predictors of treatment success.

When to use Cox Regression:
  • When analyzing time-to-event data (e.g., survival, recurrence)
  • When accounting for censored observations
  • When estimating hazard ratios for risk factors
When to use Logistic Regression:
  • When predicting a binary outcome (e.g., response vs. non-response)
  • When estimating odds ratios for risk factors
  • When building a predictive model for classification
Note: Always consider potential biases in retrospective data and interpret results cautiously.
