Statistical Analysis

Advanced statistical analysis tools for gastric oncology research and clinical decision support.

Retrospective Analysis

Analyze historical patient data to identify prognostic factors, evaluate outcomes, and generate hypotheses.

Available Methods:
  • Cox Proportional Hazards Regression - Survival analysis for time-to-event data
  • Logistic Regression - Predictive modeling for binary outcomes

Perfect for analyzing historical cohorts, identifying risk factors, and validating clinical hypotheses.

Prospective Analysis

Build predictive models to forecast patient outcomes and optimize treatment decisions.

Available Methods:
  • Random Forest - Ensemble machine learning for outcome prediction
  • Reinforcement Learning - Adaptive decision-making framework (experimental)

Ideal for personalized treatment planning, risk stratification, and clinical decision support.

Comparing Analysis Approaches

Feature Retrospective Analysis Prospective Analysis
Data Requirements Historical outcome data with follow-up Training data for model development
Primary Purpose Identify associations and risk factors Predict future outcomes
Key Methods Cox Regression, Logistic Regression Random Forest, Reinforcement Learning
Clinical Application Evidence synthesis, protocol development Treatment selection, risk stratification
Output Hazard ratios, odds ratios, p-values Predictions, probabilities, recommendations
Important Considerations
  • All statistical models have limitations and should be interpreted in the clinical context
  • Model predictions are supplements to, not replacements for, clinical judgment
  • Regular model validation and updating is essential as new evidence emerges
  • Consider biases in your data that may affect model performance and generalizability
