1. Introduction

Gastric cancer remains a significant global health challenge, with surgery representing the cornerstone of curative treatment. The integration of perioperative FLOT chemotherapy has demonstrated improved outcomes, yet optimal timing and patient selection remain areas of active investigation. This memoir presents a comprehensive analysis of our institution's experience with ADCI implementation for gastric cancer surgery with FLOT optimization.

2.3 Surgical Approach

Surgical resection was performed according to tumor location and extent. Procedures included total and subtotal gastrectomy with appropriate lymphadenectomy according to Japanese Gastric Cancer Association guidelines.

2.4 ADCI Implementation

The Adaptive Decision Confidence Index (ADCI) was implemented to optimize surgical decision-making. This framework integrates patient-specific factors, FLOT response, and institutional outcomes data to guide surgical timing and approach.

3.2 Albumin Impact Analysis

Preoperative albumin levels were identified as a significant predictor of postoperative complications. Patients with albumin levels <3.5 g/dL demonstrated a higher incidence of surgical complications, particularly infectious complications and delayed wound healing.

4. ADCI Implementation Results

Implementation of the ADCI framework demonstrated significant improvement in surgical decision precision. Key findings include:

  • Optimized timing of surgery after FLOT completion
  • Improved patient selection for extended lymphadenectomy
  • Personalized nutritional intervention for patients with low albumin levels
  • Stratified approach to minimally invasive surgery based on tumor characteristics
