Introduction

Gastric cancer remains a significant global health challenge with perioperative chemotherapy representing an important strategy to improve outcomes. The FLOT protocol (Fluorouracil, Leucovorin, Oxaliplatin, and Docetaxel) has demonstrated superiority to other regimens for locally advanced, resectable gastric or gastroesophageal junction adenocarcinoma. However, optimal timing, cycle completion, and patient selection remain areas of active investigation.

This study aims to evaluate the impact of FLOT protocol optimization on surgical outcomes through the implementation of an Adaptive Decision Confidence Index (ADCI) to guide perioperative management.

Methods

We conducted a retrospective analysis of {{ data.processed_data.cohort_size }} patients who underwent gastrectomy for gastric adenocarcinoma at our institution between January 2020 and December 2025. All patients received perioperative FLOT chemotherapy according to institutional protocols.

The ADCI framework was used to optimize decisions regarding:

  • Timing of surgery after FLOT completion
  • Nutritional optimization strategies
  • Surgical approach selection

Primary outcomes included R0 resection rate, tumor regression grade, postoperative complications, and overall survival. Statistical analysis was performed using Cox proportional hazards models and multivariable logistic regression.

Discussion

This study demonstrates the significant impact of FLOT protocol optimization on surgical outcomes in gastric cancer. Key findings include:

  1. FLOT Cycle Completion: Completion of ≥6 cycles was associated with improved tumor regression and R0 resection rates. These findings suggest that efforts should be made to support patients through the complete protocol when possible.
  2. Timing of Surgery: Optimal surgical timing was found to be 4-6 weeks after FLOT completion, balancing tumor regression with patient recovery.
  3. Nutritional Status: Preoperative albumin levels represent a modifiable risk factor that significantly impacts surgical outcomes. Nutritional optimization should be integrated into standard FLOT protocols.
  4. ADCI Implementation: The use of the ADCI framework provided objective decision support that improved surgical planning and outcomes.

Limitations of this study include its retrospective nature and single-institution design. Future prospective validation is warranted.

Conclusion

Optimization of FLOT protocol implementation through attention to cycle completion, surgical timing, and nutritional status significantly improves outcomes in gastric cancer surgery. The ADCI framework provides valuable decision support that enhances surgical precision and patient outcomes. These findings support the integration of protocol optimization and precision decision support tools in the management of gastric cancer.
